I recently picked up an Insta360 Go Ultra so I've been filming a bit more. A few videos of my son but mostly my bike rides to and from work.

Recently, on the way home from work, I captured another cyclist raging at a car who was obstructing him.

I shared the clip with a friend and they suggested I post it on the Auckland subreddit. I took a short 10 second clip, posted, went to football training for 2 hours, and when I jumped back onto the interwebs, the post had blown up.

24 hours later, the post had received over 300 comments (nothing gets /r/auckland going like a bike vs car debate).

The next day, my friend came across a repost of my video [on facebook](https://www.facebook.com/reel/1917191392233150) with over 2.8k likes and 800 comments.

People were messaging me on Reddit, asking for permission to use it on Youtube videos, it was all a bit surreal.

I forgot how the internet works. How quickly information disseminates and also how quickly people can find something to argue over.

I'm glad I posted the video. I want to create more and share.

I want to be less of a lurker and more of a creator - both on the internet and irl. Ideally I want to do it on my terms.

Perhaps one day someone will find this site as interesting as a short 10 second video - but until then I will bathe in my 2 milliseconds of fame.
